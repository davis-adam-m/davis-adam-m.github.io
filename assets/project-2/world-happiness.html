1.1. Problem Statement


For this project, we will be evaluating a cleaned and merged dataset derived from three separate original datasets that each provide complementary data on global countries over a range of years for a number of indicators, including happiness scores, educational benchmarks, and population metrics. The primary question we are looking to answer: what patterns surface when evaluating the association between happiness scores and metrics of population/education?
 
#### 1. World Happiness Report Data

Reason for selection: This dataset is an interesting perspective on scoring world happiness by country, based on a detailed and rigorous survey and statistical method. It will be a good basis for combining with other country-based data and fits the project requirements, having at least 2 variables, greater than 500 samples (defined as observations per country/year pair), and contains data quality and tidiness issues to be corrected. Manual download was chosen as the gathering method for this dataset, as the raw data is made available for direct download on the publisher's website.

Method: The data was downloaded manually for this source, as raw data is made available for direct download from https://www.worldhappiness.report/data-sharing/.

* Explained by: Perceptions of corruption
